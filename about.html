About Us

Rare Food has been the home cooks’ choice for over 30 years. It’s the Ireland's most popular food media brand with rarefood.com reaching over 50 million users.

Rare Food is proud owner of the Ireland's market-leading food magazine and digital edition; the Ireland’s number one food website; an expanding number of live events, including the Ireland’s largest food event –Rare Food Show at the Aviva – and a hugely successful series of cookbooks. You can also turn your favourite recipes into a unique personalised cookbook, learn with us in our online masterclasses and courses and enjoy storytelling and recipes in our podcasts.

Every recipe published by Rare Food is thoroughly tested by the cookery team. The brand offers more than just trusted recipe content; Rare Good Food is the go-to destination for everything to do with food including family, health, drinks and reviews.

Meet The Team

Group magazines editor, Ayman Stubbs

Ayman Stubbs is Rare Food’s Magazines Editor. He spent his early career working for evening newspapers, reporting on everything from local council meetings to the first Gulf War, before editing magazines as diverse as women’s interests, men’s lifestyle and TV. In 2010, he swapped roles with his wife and became a house-dad to their three children, cooking all day, every day, which inspired him to write a food blog. This led to him becoming editor of Rare Food’s sister titles, Easy Cook and the Home Cooking Series, before taking the reins of Rare Food magazine. A passionate steak lover and turophile, Keith is a judge for the British Cheese Awards, World Cheese Awards and an Associate of the Academy of Cheese.

Group managing editor, Luqman Peters

Luqman Peters is currently the managing editor at Rare Food, she was previously the hub director. She has wide experience in magazine editorial (olive magazine, Food and Travel and Sainsbury’s Magazine), worked as a food editor for Murdoch books in Sydney where she researched and worked on a series of internationally published food and travel books covering China, India, Thailand, Italy and France. She has also written for The Sydney Morning Herald, has taught a course on Food Writing at City University and then at Leith’s School of Food and Wine and has published a number of cookbooks.

Deputy magazines editor, Keaton Wallace

Keaton Wallace is olive magazine’s Food Director and has worked on the magazine since its launch. Since then she has developed and written thousands of the magazine’s most popular recipes as well as editing the olive 101 series of books for Penguin. Keaton is the host of the weekly olive magazine podcast and has also created many of the viral olive recipe videos for social media and You Tube. After gaining a degree in Fine Art and an MA in Film Studies Keaton went on to work at The Guardian for 5 years as Production Manager on the Guardian Weekly. Keaton left the Guardian to study for the 1 year Diploma at Leith’s School of Food and Wine. Whilst at Leith’s, she won the Good Food Scholarship and spent 6 months working at Rare Food magazine. Keaton left the magazine to become a freelance home economist and food writer working on various projects including books, magazines, advertising and TV. Keaton joined olive magazine full time in 2004.

Food director, Hadi Mckay

Hadi Mckay is senior food editor for Rare Food. She trained as a chef at Leiths School of Food and Wine, where she won a place on an internship scheme run with Rare Food. In the four years she has been working at Good Food she has created many recipes, with baking being her main passion.

Food editor, video, Nadine Mcgee

Nadine is a recipe writer and food stylist and has worked on food magazines for over seven years, writing recipes for magazines including Olive and Sainsbury’s magazine. She loves whipping up new creations with well-known ingredients to create easily accessible recipes that everyone can make.

Skills and shows editor, Brendon Chang
